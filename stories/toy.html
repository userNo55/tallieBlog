My boyfriend works as a programmer, but he's always been drawn to "Illusions." This technology became popular immediately after its invention because it allowed one to safely experience any scenario with a full set of physical sensations. Naturally, this also led to dangerous experiments. Therefore, creating "Illusions" with elements of any kind of violence was banned in almost all countries.


The precedent was a mass murder of nineteen people, committed with particular cruelty by a twenty-three-year-old man who had previously been sitting around the clock in Illusions with scenes of dismemberment. At the trial, he admitted that he wanted to know what it was like in reality. Journalists attribute the following phrase to him: "In reality, everything is much cooler," adding that this part was forbidden to be recorded and distributed.


In any case, the incident became a starting point for studying the psyche of normal people and those prone to violence. They forced both types of people to sit in "Illusions" with various types of violence for 2 to 8 hours a day. Analysis of the results showed that after such entertainment, both categories exhibited violence more often and more intensely, regardless of who it was directed at in the "Illusions." Although many other studies were subsequently conducted that did not show such a correlation. And despite the fact that in some cases, violence in "Illusions" had a positive effect on people's mental state, for example, helping to relieve stress, the ban was introduced. There was no longer any need to think, analyze, or decide, although there was no one to blame either. Nothing could be done.


Stress was recommended to be relieved by breaking virtual cars and dishes, as the ban only applied to violence against living beings or objects that were once alive. Even a cooking simulator fell under this ban – the part where you had to gut a fish. It was eventually removed and ready-made fillets were added for cooking.


And so my boyfriend decided to make money from this. In his megacorporation, he works on sensors that analyze human consciousness. For medical purposes, of course. As part of a device that creates an ideal comfortable place for a specific user, where they can relax and unwind from stressful productive work of 18 hours a day.


My guy, Peter, decided to use the information read from the brain differently. His version of the sensor looked for what could give a person not peace, but the highest pleasure in any form. He copied part of the code from the medical sensor and rewrote the rest himself. Secretly. I keep waiting for him to get caught, but Peter just laughs. He says the people who work on such projects are romantic idealists. They know people lie, but they can't imagine that people lie to them.


Previously, all "Illusions" were created manually like video games or converted from recorded real-life videos. Scenario development required serious financial backing but allowed earning thousands of times more. It became another kind of drug.


Peter's technology, on the other hand, allowed generating an infinite amount of content without any restrictions. He was already thinking about a plan to sell a couple of his devices to celebrities and corporate executives. The main thing is not to run into his boss. Then hide the profits, quietly quit, and take me somewhere nice. Classic.


He hasn't decided yet where exactly, but he's sure that "with that kind of money, you don't need to plan." I think the development process gives him much more pleasure than thinking about what he'll get from the results. In this, he's a typical programmer. Until this evening, I didn't expect him to actually finish it.


I came home from work at one in the morning, as usual. After showering, I only wanted one thing – to go to sleep. To avoid wasting melatonin, I didn't turn on the light and navigated the dark apartment by the neon strips on the floor. When I was drying my hair, Peter appeared before me with a creepy figurine in his hands. Its design reminded me of the once-popular wooden bear sculptures symbolizing depression, irony, and criticism of capitalism. Peter's toy was made of metal and had bizarre discs at the ends of its paws. My attention was drawn away from the discs by the many small details like chains and ribbons that entangled the bear from head to toe. It was supposed to look like contemporary art.


"I'm done," Peter whispered in my ear. "Wanna try?"


"I'm tired and just want to sleep."


"Come on, I've already tested it on myself, and it works amazingly. When you try it, you'll slap me for every second of delay. I've already reset it for you." I didn't like arguing with him, so I quickly gave in.


"Well, I've freshened up; maybe a little pleasure won't hurt?"


Peter followed me into the bedroom. I settled into the "Illusion" chair and placed my head between the discs in the hands of the creepy beast doll. The disc-electrodes attached to my temples, and the bear released smoke from its mouth into my face. I closed my eyes and plunged into the "Illusion."


Darkness surrounded me. I walked forward, but nothing changed. Then the smell of a pine forest appeared, and the forest instantly surrounded me. The scents of flowers and berries filled everything around, but the roses, strawberries, raspberries, blueberries themselves were not there. The mixture was so beautiful and enticing that I couldn't stop. I breathed and breathed until I felt dizzy. I fell and found myself on a soft bed with fresh cotton sheets. This clean, fresh smell displaced the storm of intoxicating aromas. Only now did I realize I wasn't wearing any clothes. The starched, slightly stiff blanket touched my skin pleasantly. Without realizing it, I wrapped myself in it, and it began to hug and caress me. Closing my eyes, I moaned with pleasure. But the next second, I felt embarrassed, and immediately the blanket and the forest vanished into darkness, and a naked man appeared above me. He was so beautiful that if I had met him in reality, I would have given him all my money and converted to any religion just to spend one more moment with him. As if hypnotized, I watched his every move, his tense muscles, his facial expressions, and I couldn't think of anything but a passionate plea – don't go, stay, touch me.


He was here especially for me, and it would stay that way until I changed my mind. This thought excited me. He kissed me, then my neck, my chest, moving lower and lower. He did everything I loved. Precisely at the moment it gave me the most pleasure.


When a knife appeared in his hand, fear and excitement mixed into a frenzy. The rules of the real world still restrained my mind, but not my subconscious. The man ran the sharp knife with its shiny, elegant blade along my thigh. Pain. Tolerable pain. He continued to leave shallow, short cuts on the inside of my thighs. As we moved, I felt the edges of the wounds rub against each other in the same rhythm. In reality, I would never have allowed this to happen to me. The danger to health and life, the fear of a person capable of doing such a thing to another person, to me – everything would have stopped it immediately. But the excitement and ecstasy I was experiencing were intoxicating. This wasn't reality. This thought freed and destroyed the last walls. My consciousness dissolved more and more into this forbidden pleasure. I didn't want to exist if it all stopped. Only here. Only now. My lover raised the knife over me. And I wanted it.


"Please!" I screamed in a frenzy. The knife flashed, so cold and pleasant.


I woke up. I opened my eyes. I felt nauseous, my body didn't obey. Peter was sitting next to me.


"How did it go? Judging by the sensors, it was amazing, but I want to hear it from you!" He smiled so wide the edges of his gums showed.


"Great."


I never touched that toy again. I don't need that ultimate pleasure.


Not yet.
